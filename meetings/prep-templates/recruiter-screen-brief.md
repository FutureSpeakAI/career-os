## Salary Deflection Script

When the recruiter asks about comp expectations (and they will, usually within the first 10 minutes):

### First deflection:
> "I'm focused on finding the right role and the right team first. Compensation is important, but I'd rather understand the scope and what success looks like before we anchor on numbers. What's the budgeted range for this position?"

### If they push:
> "I'm targeting $[TARGET RANGE] total comp based on market data for this role level. I'm flexible on structure; what matters is the total package and the opportunity. What does the full picture look like on your side?"

### If they ask current comp:
> "I'd rather not anchor this conversation to my current compensation. I'm focused on what this role is worth in the market. Let's talk about what this position pays."

### Rules:
- Never disclose your current comp
- Never reference your current employer's comp structure
- If they won't share their range and you must go first, anchor 15-20% above your target
- Match their energy: if they're transactional, be direct; if they're conversational, be warm but firm

## Questions to Qualify the Role

Ask at least 3 of these. The recruiter screen is as much about you qualifying them as them qualifying you.

### Must-Ask:
1. **"Is this a new role or a backfill?"**
   *Why:* New role = you're defining it. Backfill = someone left, and you need to know why.

2. **"Who does this role report to?"**
   *Why:* Reporting to the CEO/CTO = real mandate. Reporting to a VP of Marketing = AI is a support function, not a strategic priority.

3. **"What does the interview process look like from here?"**
   *Why:* 2-3 rounds = decisive company. 6+ rounds = committee culture, slow decisions.

4. **"What's the timeline for filling this role?"**
   *Why:* "Yesterday" = fire drill, desperation. "Q3" = they're building a pipeline and you may wait months. "Next 3-4 weeks" = healthy urgency.

### Good to Ask:
5. **"How large is the current AI team, if there is one?"**
   *Why:* Tells you if you're building from zero or scaling an existing function.

6. **"Is the role remote, or is there an expectation of on-site time?"**
   *Why:* Clarify now. "Hybrid" means different things to different companies.

## Red Flags to Listen For

Watch for these signals during the conversation. Any one of them warrants deeper diligence before investing more time.

| Red Flag | What It Means |
|----------|--------------|
| Recruiter can't explain what the AI team does | AI is a checkbox, not a priority |
| "We're looking for someone to wear many hats" | Understaffed; you'll be a one-person team |
| Comp range starts below $[YOUR FLOOR - $25K] | Below your floor; likely not competitive |
| "The last person left after [short tenure]" | Something is broken; find out what |
| Vague on reporting structure | The role hasn't been designed; you're defining it in real-time |
| "We're still figuring out our AI strategy" | They want you to figure it out, possibly without budget or authority |
| Recruiter pushes hard on current salary | Cost-driven hire, not value-driven |
| No mention of team, budget, or hiring authority | This is an IC role disguised as a leadership role |

## When They Ask: "Why Are You Looking?"

> "I've had an incredible run at [Current Company]. [Brief highlights: key promotions, accomplishments, scope of work]. But I've reached a point where my career goals require a different kind of opportunity. I'm looking for a role where I can own the AI strategy at a larger scale, build a team, and ship products that define how an organization operates. That's the next chapter I'm focused on."

### Rules:
- Never badmouth your current employer or leadership
- Never mention the comp gap as the primary reason
- Focus on what you're running toward, not what you're running from
- Keep it under 30 seconds
