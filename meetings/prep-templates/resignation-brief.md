# Resignation Brief

**Meeting with:** [Manager's name and title]
**Date/Time:** [DATE] at [TIME]
**Format:** [Video call / In-person]

## Objective

Resign professionally. Protect the relationship. Leave on strong terms.

## Opening Script

> "[Manager's name], I want to have an honest conversation with you. I've made the decision to resign from [Company]. My last day would be [date]. I want to handle the transition professionally and make sure the team is set up for success."

## Talking Points

### If asked "Why?"

> "This isn't about any single thing. I've valued the work here and the growth I've experienced. But I've reached a point where my career goals require a different kind of opportunity. I owe it to myself to pursue that."

### Rules of engagement
- Do not compare current company to the new opportunity
- Do not criticize leadership, comp, or culture
- Express genuine gratitude for growth and opportunities
- Be firm but warm
- Do not negotiate in the moment

### If asked "What would change your mind?"

> "I appreciate you asking. I've thought about this carefully and I'm confident in my decision. I'd rather focus our time together on making the transition as smooth as possible."

## Counter-Offer Framework

If a counter-offer is made, take 48 hours. Do not decide in the meeting.

### Counter-offer evaluation criteria

| Dimension | Minimum to consider |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Base salary | [Your walk-away number from profile.yml] |
| Title / scope | [Level you want] |
| Team / reports | [What you need] |
| Timeline | [When changes take effect] |
| Written | Must be documented, not verbal |

**The key question:** If they had offered this counter 6 months ago, unprompted, would you have stayed? If yes, the counter might be genuine. If no, they're buying time.

## Transition Plan

Offer these proactively:

- [ ] Document current projects and handoff notes
- [ ] Transfer admin access and credentials
- [ ] Brief team members on ongoing work
- [ ] Complete any critical deliverables before departure
- [ ] Make yourself available for questions during notice period

## Emotional Preparation

Expect these feelings to surface during the conversation:
- Guilt (especially if your manager has been supportive)
- Nostalgia (real accomplishments and relationships worth honoring)
- Second-guessing (normal; this is why you decided in advance)

Grounding statement:
- "Gratitude and departure are not contradictions. I can be thankful for what this role gave me and still know it's time to go."
