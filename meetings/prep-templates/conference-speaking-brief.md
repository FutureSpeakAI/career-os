## Talk Structure Outline

### For a Keynote (20-30 minutes):

**Opening (2-3 minutes):**
[Provocative declaration or concrete anecdote. Not "thank you for having me." Start with the thing that makes them lean forward.]

Example opener: "[A compelling opening line drawn from your unique career background that leads into your thesis.]"

**Act 1: The Problem (5-7 minutes):**
[Establish the challenge the audience is facing. Use specific numbers and examples. Make them feel seen.]

**Act 2: The Insight (8-10 minutes):**
[Your unique perspective. The thing they can't get from anyone else. This is where your signature project stories and research findings live.]

**Act 3: The Path Forward (5-7 minutes):**
[Practical implications. What they should do differently starting tomorrow. Be specific enough to be actionable but not so prescriptive that it feels like a consulting pitch.]

**Closing (2-3 minutes):**
[The memorable landing. Loop back to the opening. Land the thesis one more time with a reframe that elevates it.]

### For a Panel (varies):

**Your opening statement (60-90 seconds):**
[Crisp, memorable, establishes your angle. Not your resume; your perspective.]

**3 prepared talking points:**
1. [Point you'll make regardless of what they ask]
2. [Point that differentiates you from the other panelists]
3. [Point that's slightly contrarian; the thing nobody else will say]

**2 bridges:**
- "That's an interesting point, and it connects to something I've seen in practice..."
- "I'd push back on that slightly, because my experience in [your domain] shows..."

### For a Fireside Chat:

**3 stories ready to tell:**
1. [Short version of your signature enterprise transformation story, 2 minutes]
2. [Short version of a key research or product story, 2 minutes]
3. [Short version of your career pivot or origin story, 2 minutes]

**1 unexpected anecdote:**
[Something personal, surprising, or funny that humanizes you. A self-deprecating failure. A moment of unexpected discovery. Something memorable.]
