# Rejection Tracker

Track every rejection to find patterns, refine targeting, and turn losses into data.

---

## How to Use

1. Log every rejection the day you receive it -- including ghosting (mark as "No response after X weeks")
2. Be brutally honest in the "Real Reason" column -- your assessment matters more than their stated reason
3. Review the "Pattern?" column monthly -- if you see the same pattern 3+ times, it's a signal to change something
4. The "Adjustment Made" column closes the loop -- every pattern should produce an action

### Stage Rejected Values
- **Auto-Screen** -- ATS rejected, never saw a human
- **Resume Review** -- Recruiter reviewed and passed
- **Recruiter Screen** -- Phone screen with recruiter, didn't advance
- **Hiring Manager Screen** -- HM conversation, didn't advance
- **Technical Interview** -- Failed a technical or case-study round
- **Panel Interview** -- Multiple interviewers, didn't advance
- **Final Round** -- Made it to the end, lost to another candidate
- **Offer Withdrawn** -- Had an offer or verbal, company pulled back
- **Ghosted (2wk)** -- No response after 2 weeks post-submission
- **Ghosted (4wk)** -- No response after 4 weeks post-submission
- **Ghosted (post-interview)** -- Interviewed, then silence

### Common "Real Reasons" to Consider
- They wanted hands-on ML engineering, not AI strategy
- CS degree / PhD was a hard filter
- Internal candidate was already chosen
- Role was put on hold / headcount frozen
- Comp expectations didn't align (they wanted cheaper)
- Location requirement was firm despite listing saying "remote"
- My narrative didn't translate to their industry
- The role wasn't what the JD described
- I wasn't senior enough for the scope
- I was overqualified for the actual scope
- Interview performance issue (specific: ___)
- Cultural mismatch surfaced during interviews

## Rejection Benchmarks

These are typical rejection rates for executive-level AI roles. Knowing the baseline prevents you from interpreting normal attrition as personal failure.

| Stage | Typical Pass Rate | Your Pass Rate | Notes |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|-------|
| Application to Recruiter Screen | 10-20% | ___% | Cold apps are brutal; warm intros 3-5x this rate |
| Recruiter Screen to HM Screen | 40-60% | ___% | If below 40%, your pitch needs tightening |
| HM Screen to Interview Loop | 50-70% | ___% | If below 50%, the role or narrative isn't connecting |
| Interview Loop to Offer | 25-50% | ___% | Finalist rounds are competitive; 1 in 3 is normal |
| End-to-end (App to Offer) | 2-5% | ___% | At 84 apps, expect 2-4 offers if pipeline is healthy |

**Key insight:** Even at a 3% conversion rate, 84 tracked roles should eventually yield 2-3 offers -- but only if they're submitted. The current bottleneck is applications sitting in "Evaluated" or "Packaged" status without being sent.

### Ghost Analysis Questions (Review Monthly)
- Are ghosted applications concentrated in a specific industry?
- Are ghosted applications mostly cold apps vs. referrals?
- Does application method matter? (Portal vs. email vs. LinkedIn Easy Apply)
- Is there a company size pattern? (Startups ghost less? Enterprises ghost more?)

## Rejection Response Templates

### Requesting Feedback After Rejection
> Thank you for letting me know, [name]. I appreciate the time your team invested in our conversations.
>
> If you're able to share, I'd welcome any feedback on where my profile didn't align -- it would help me focus my search. Either way, I enjoyed learning about [specific project/initiative discussed] and wish the team well.

### After Being Ghosted (4+ Weeks)
> Hi [name], I wanted to follow up on my application for the [Role] position submitted on [date]. I understand timelines shift, and if the role has been filled or put on hold, no worries at all -- I'd just appreciate a quick update so I can plan accordingly. Thank you!

### Turning a Rejection into a Future Lead
> I appreciate the update, [name]. While this particular role wasn't the right fit, I'm very interested in [Company] and the work your AI team is doing. Would you be open to keeping my profile on file for future roles in AI strategy or leadership? I'm also happy to connect on LinkedIn to stay in touch.
